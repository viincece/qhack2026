# Capabilities Overview

## Summary

Meridian Intelligence GmbH offers a suite of integrated capabilities centred on large-scale market and ecosystem mapping for EU institutional clients. The company combines web-scale data engineering, NLP-based classification, supply chain analysis, and regulatory scoping to deliver structured intelligence on niche and fragmented markets. Over 40 projects have been completed, processing more than 80 million organisational web records.

## Core Capability Areas

### 1. Market & Ecosystem Mapping

- Scale: 10,000 to 500,000 entities per project
- Covers organisational identification, profiling, and categorisation
- Draws on web data, registry data, and specialised databases
- Applicable to any sector where standard databases have coverage gaps
- Delivered for cybersecurity ([[projects/enisa_2023_cybersecurity]]), data economy ([[projects/jrc_2024_data_economy]]), FinTech ([[projects/eba_2024_fintech_dora]]), deep tech ([[projects/dg_grow_2022_deep_tech]]), media ([[projects/dg_cnect_2023_media]]), and AI ([[projects/bmwi_2021_ai_startups]])

### 2. Evidence-Based Classification

- NLP/ML classifiers assign relevance scores and multi-label categories
- F1 performance: 84--91% across domains (see [[methodology/classification_approach]])
- Classifiers trained per-domain: cybersecurity (F1 0.87), data economy (F1 0.88), AI signal (F1 0.85), FinTech (F1 0.85)
- Multi-label taxonomy support for complex regulatory definitions

### 3. Continuous Monitoring

- Frequency: quarterly to monthly update cycles
- Rolling coverage: 500,000+ entities under active monitoring
- Change detection and versioned datasets with semantic versioning
- Suitable for observatory-type contracts and multi-year frameworks

### 4. Supply Chain Mapping

- Scale: up to 50,000 relationship edges per project
- Maps inter-organisational relationships: supplier, customer, partner, investor
- Supports concentration analysis and dependency assessment
- Applied in CRA product category mapping ([[projects/enisa_2023_cybersecurity]]) and platform dependency analysis ([[projects/dg_cnect_2023_media]])

### 5. Regulatory Scoping

- Translates legal definitions into operational classification criteria
- Bridges policy language and data-driven entity identification
- Applied to DORA ([[projects/eba_2024_fintech_dora]]), CRA ([[projects/enisa_2023_cybersecurity]]), Data Act/DGA ([[projects/jrc_2024_data_economy]])
- Enables policy-aligned taxonomies that map directly to regulatory requirements
