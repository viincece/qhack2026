# Past Response: EBA/2024/OP/0003 — FinTech and Digital Finance Market Analysis

## Client
European Banking Authority (EBA), Paris

## Executive Summary (as submitted)
Meridian Intelligence proposes a structured mapping and analysis of third-party ICT providers (TTPs) subject to DORA, focusing on critical third-party providers (CTPPs) falling under direct EBA oversight. Approach combines evidence-based entity identification, DORA-aligned entity typology, and structured concentration analysis. Output: structured dataset of 6,400+ verified ICT providers with indicator profiles. Deliverable within 10 months.

## Problem Framing (reusable pattern)
- DORA's concentration risk provisions require understanding supply chain dependencies
- Article 29 DORA requires EBA to maintain a register of CTPPs
- Challenge: ICT providers to financial entities are NOT a self-identifying population
- Standard approaches (survey-based, registry-based) systematically undercount niche and emerging providers
- Meridian's evidence-based approach addresses this population identification problem

## Methodology Proposed
- [[webmap_pipeline]] adapted for DORA scope
- Entity typology designed by [[sofia_chen]] for DORA-relevant ICT providers
- Classification: FinTech/DORA ICT provider classifier (F1: 0.85)
- Concentration analysis: map supplier-customer relationships
