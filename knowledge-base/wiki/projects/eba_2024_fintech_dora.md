# EBA/2024/OP/0003 -- FinTech/DORA Market Analysis

## Summary

A market analysis of third-party ICT providers falling under the Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA), commissioned by the European Banking Authority (EBA). The project mapped 6,400+ entities, developed a DORA-aligned entity typology, and conducted concentration analysis to assess systemic risk from ICT provider dependencies in the financial sector.

## Deliverables

1. **Entity typology**: DORA-aligned classification framework for third-party ICT providers
2. **Entity dataset**: 6,400+ verified ICT provider entities
3. **Concentration analysis**: Assessment of systemic concentration risk in ICT service provision to the financial sector
4. **Policy brief**: Targeted briefing for EBA policymakers

## Challenge & Approach

**Core challenge**: Third-party ICT providers regulated under DORA do not self-identify as such. There is no existing registry or database of "DORA-relevant ICT providers" -- the population must be constructed from evidence.

**Approach**: Adapted the [[methodology/webmap_pipeline]] for the DORA scope. [[team/sofia_chen]] translated DORA's legal definitions into operational classification criteria. NLP classifiers (FinTech domain F1: 0.85, see [[team/marcus_weber]]) identified relevant entities from web evidence. The entity typology was developed to categorise providers by service type, enabling concentration analysis.

Delivery was completed within 10 months (ahead of the 12-month contract period).
