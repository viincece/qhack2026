# ENISA/2023/OP/0008 -- Cybersecurity SME Landscape

## Summary

A systematic mapping of the EU cybersecurity SME landscape across all 27 member states, commissioned by the European Union Agency for Cybersecurity (ENISA). The project identified and verified 14,200+ cybersecurity entities with indicator profiles, mapped supply chains across 8 CRA (Cyber Resilience Act) product categories, and delivered quarterly updates over an 18-month period.

## Deliverables

1. **Entity dataset**: 14,200+ verified cybersecurity entities across EU-27, with indicator profiles
2. **Supply chain map**: Relationship mapping across 8 CRA product categories
3. **Quarterly updates**: 4 quarterly update cycles with change tracking
4. **Final report**: Comprehensive analytical report on the EU cybersecurity SME landscape

## Challenge & Approach

**Core challenge**: The "population problem" -- cybersecurity SMEs do not reliably self-classify in standard business databases. Standard sources (Orbis, Crunchbase) miss niche players, especially in smaller member states.

**Approach**: Applied the [[methodology/webmap_pipeline]] to systematically identify entities via web evidence. NLP classifiers (cybersecurity domain F1: 0.87, see [[team/marcus_weber]]) assigned relevance scores. Supply chain mapping captured up to 50,000 relationship edges across CRA categories. The project was aligned with the European Cybersecurity Skills Framework (ECSMAF).
