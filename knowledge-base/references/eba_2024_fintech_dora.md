# FinTech and Digital Finance Market Analysis: Third-Party ICT Providers under DORA

## Technical Tender

**Contracting authority**
European Banking Authority (EBA) — Paris

## Executive Summary

Meridian Intelligence GmbH proposes a structured mapping and analysis of third-party ICT providers (TTPs) subject to the Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA), with particular focus on the population of critical third-party providers (CTPPs) that will fall under direct EBA oversight. Our approach combines evidence-based entity identification, DORA-aligned entity typology, and a structured concentration analysis to give EBA a clear, empirically grounded picture of the ICT supply chain dependencies of EU financial entities.

The output is a structured dataset of 6,400+ verified ICT providers serving EU financial sector entities, with indicator profiles covering: service category, client sector concentration, geographic footprint, and CTPP designation likelihood. Deliverable within 10 months of contract signature.

## 1. Problem Framing

DORA's concentration risk provisions require financial entities and their supervisors to understand the degree to which critical functions depend on a small number of third-party ICT providers. Article 29 DORA requires EBA to maintain a register of CTPPs designated by the Joint Oversight Committee. Building that register — and keeping it current — requires knowing who the relevant providers are.

The challenge is that ICT providers to financial entities are not a self-identifying population. A cloud infrastructure provider may serve financial clients alongside thousands of others and not describe itself as a 'financial sector ICT provider.' A payment technology company may be critical infrastructure for dozens of banks without appearing in any financial sector registry.

Meridian's approach solves this by identifying financial sector relevance through observable evidence: procurement records, job postings for roles specific to financial sector clients, technology partnership announcements, and direct references to financial clients on provider websites.

## 3. Methodology

### 3.1 Identification
Seed universe: 800,000+ ICT service providers in EU27, drawn from company registries (NACE J58–J63, K64–K66 for payment services) and Meridian's web index. Evidence classification applied to identify financial sector relevance. Target precision: ≥97% (false-positive rate <3%).

### 3.2 Concentration Analysis
For identified providers, we will analyse:
- Client breadth: estimated number and type of EU financial entities served (by AUM tier, jurisdiction, business model)
- Service criticality: share of financial sector revenue derived from the provider's core service category
- Substitutability: existence of credible alternative providers for the same service (based on market share data and technical specification matching)
- Geographic concentration: proportion of critical services delivered from a single jurisdiction or data centre cluster

### 3.3 CTPP Likelihood Scoring
Each identified provider will receive a CTPP likelihood score (0–100) based on: estimated number of EU financial entities served; estimated share of systemic functions covered; degree of substitutability; and geographic concentration risk. This score is advisory — formal CTPP designation is an EBA/JOC decision — but provides a ranked shortlist for supervisory prioritisation.
